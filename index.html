Core Thesis

Argues for sustainable competency-oriented agile education to cultivate Π-shaped elites capable of adapting to rapidly changing digital environments.

Competency-first
📋

CC2020 Alignment

Aligns with CC2020 (IEEE CS + ACM) defining competency as job-relevant behavior, motivation, and technical knowledge/skills for effective performance.

IEEE CS + ACM
📊

Key Definition

Sustainable competence refers to adaptability to future social changes, creativity for innovation, and the operation ability to drive social and technological progress.
— Blue Book, 2019

Proposed at the Twenty-Experts Forum on Computer Education in China — foundational to rethinking computing curricula.

Talent Frameworks

From I-shaped specialists to Π-shaped comprehensive leaders

I

I-Shaped People

Deep specialist in a single domain. Traditional model for computing graduates — strong vertically, limited in breadth.

Specialist only
T

T-Shaped Elites

Broad knowledge paired with one deep specialty. Diverse abilities and strong comprehensive qualities across disciplines.

Breadth + depth
Target Model
Π

Π-Shaped Leading Talents

Two deep specialties, broad foundation, plus industrial experience. Leadership with interdisciplinary mastery.

Dual depth + industry
⚙️

Agile Education Model

  • Multi-round iteration of knowledge learning and ability promotion cycles
  • Concurrent theory & practice — rapid co-learning of both
  • Multi-university coordination for diverse educational resources
  • University-industry collaboration bringing enterprise cases into classrooms
🌐

Open Education Ecosystem

  • Student-centric environment coordinating internal & external resources
  • International resource integration via multi-channel collaboration
  • Governance & compliance as core operational factors
  • Promotion & constraint roles in education system evolution
